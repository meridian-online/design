Relief rule: three light-mode slots sit below 3:1 against the surface (the reference palette carries the same count) — legal with visible direct labels or the table view. Dark mode: all eight clear 3:1. Series cap: the first four slots validate all-pairs (scatter, choropleth) at CVD ΔE 9.9 — past four, fold to “Other”, facet, or label directly.

Call ① — Sequential default: Meridian blue, or keep viridis?

The Meridian ramp is Maritime-anchored (OKLCH hue 240, 13 steps, ordinal-validated in both modes). Viridis is the current Brightfield default — familiar, scientific, but generic and green-yellow against warm neutrals. Both shown as heatmap strips on the real surfaces.

Either way, viridis / blues / turbo stay available as named schemes — this call sets only the default a bare spec renders with. A second concurrent sequential context takes the next categorical hue as its own one-hue ramp.

Call ② — Diverging warm pole: burnt orange, or brick red?

Cool pole is Maritime blue in both candidates; the midpoint is the warm neutral (light #f4f3f2, dark #2a2928), so “nothing” reads as nothing. Orange echoes the warm ground and keeps red free for status/critical; red is the stronger conventional signal for negative/positive.

Status & null ink

Status colours are reserved — never reused as series — and always ship with icon + label. null_ink gives NULL values a warm-gray cell that cannot impersonate a scheme value (it closes Brightfield’s NULL-reads-as-high-value bug).
